Verdilex Transcription Services – FAQs

How do I request a criminal transcript?
You can request a criminal transcript by completing our online request form or emailing info@verdilextranscription.com. Please include case details, court location, and hearing date.
How do I request a transcript for a Civil or Family court matter, or Tribunal?
Submit your request through our secure online form or email info@verdilextranscription.com. Provide relevant case numbers, court type, and hearing details to ensure accurate processing.
Who are the regional court transcript providers?
Verdilex serves clients across the U.S., providing transcripts for federal, state, and local courts. Contact us directly at info@verdilextranscription.com to confirm service availability in your region.
I am having issues downloading the transcript request form. What should I do?
If you cannot download the form, try using a different browser or device. Alternatively, email info@verdilextranscription.com and we will provide the form directly.
What is a Transcript at Public Expense?
A transcript at public expense is a court transcript paid for by the state for eligible parties who cannot afford the cost, such as defendants in criminal cases. Contact your local court for eligibility details.
How do I get a copy of an existing transcript?
Email info@verdilextranscription.com with the case number and court details. We will provide instructions for requesting copies of existing transcripts.
What delivery turnarounds are available?
Delivery times vary based on transcript length and urgency. Standard delivery is 5–7 business days, while expedited delivery options are available for an additional fee.
What can impact the delivery time/date of my transcript?
Factors include court scheduling delays, transcript length, audio quality, and high demand periods. Providing accurate and complete information can help ensure timely delivery.
Can I get a transcript if my hearing was held by telephone or online?
Yes. Verdilex can provide transcripts for remote hearings if clear audio recordings are available.
How much will my transcript cost?
Costs vary based on transcript type, length, and delivery speed. Contact info@verdilextranscription.com for an accurate quote.
What is a Reporting Restriction?
A reporting restriction limits public access to certain court proceedings or documents. Verdilex complies fully with all applicable reporting restrictions.
How can I track the progress of my order?
You will receive an email confirmation with a tracking number once your transcript order is processed. You can check the status via our portal or by contacting us directly at info@verdilextranscription.com.
How do I lodge a complaint about Verdilex’s service?
If you have any concerns or complaints, please email info@verdilextranscription.com with full details, and our support team will respond promptly.
